Mysterious yana Rocks & Caves

July 22, 2019 4 Comments

22nd July 2018

(Two magnificent hillocks…., surrounded by thick forests and streams, rise sharply above the surrounding area.. unbelievable n thrilling..).

Sometimes unexpected things surprises us more same happened with me. Actually we were on Gokarna trip n there I got information about Yanna rocks and we planned as it was on return route.. I have no any expectations From this place just though it may be routine cave and rock formation

"Always Became Frisky mad about life" POOJA PATEL;TRAVELLER

We left Gokarna @8AM after having breakfast and tea. Uttara Kannad district is blessed with nature ..bordered by Arabian sea n nestled in sahyadri mountain range. Around 9am our car joined sirsi Yanna road and literally Literally we disconnected from the world.. No network for cellphone ..most of the sign boards in Kannada language.

Dense forest Circular empty roads.. everyone is little scared inside and me too. But also enjoying the scenic drive. At such situation I love to turn off AC n roll down window glasses. That natural unpolluted air is refreshing always. Finally we arrived parking area of yana rock. From here we have to walk around 1 and half km. It’s very remote place very few people was there. It’s not safe for solo travelers. I was really enjoying the forest vibe.. jungle was so dense that sunlight was unable to reach ground at many areas.

We started walk after having coconut water. It’s killing.. birds chuckling on background monkeys and peacocks are repeated visitors by path.After a walk of 10 minutes two massive grey rocks emerged from greenery are visible. When I arrived at the destination the view was breathtaking..Yana is famous for these two massive rock outcrops known as the Bhairaveshwara Shikhara and the Mohini Shikhara (“Shikhara” means “hill”). The huge rocks are composed of solid black, crystalline limestone.. Bhairaveshwara Shikhara is 120 meters (390 ft) in height, while the Mohini Shikhara, which is smaller, is 90 meters (300 ft) in height. Yana is also well known as a pilgrimage center because of the cave temple below the Bhairaveshwara where a swayambhu (“self manifested”, or “that which is created by its own accord”) linga as been formed. Water drips from the roof over the linga, adding to the sanctity of the place..I wondered there around two hours.. discovered cave n surrounding area.. it’s so mysterious. Nobody knows how this formation took place.

After wondering about two hours we started our return journey with a fullfillment. One can also visit Jog falls nearby but advised in rainy season. This place is for pure nature lover. There are not much food options and all.But views you get are mesmerizing that one I can promise you.. bye see you in Gokarn blog soon..
